Kachori Preparation

Ingredients:

  1. 1 cup all-purpose flour (maida)
  2. 1/4 cup semolina (sooji)
  3. 2 tablespoons ghee or oil
  4. 1/4 teaspoon baking soda
  5. 1/4 teaspoon salt
  6. Water, as needed, to make the dough
  7. For the filling:
  8. 1 cup yellow moong dal, soaked for 2 hours
  9. 1 teaspoon cumin seeds
  10. 1 teaspoon fennel seeds
  11. 1/2 teaspoon turmeric powder
  12. 1/2 teaspoon red chili powder
  13. 1/2 teaspoon garam masala
  14. 1 tablespoon grated ginger
  15. 2 tablespoons chopped coriander leaves
  16. Salt to taste
  17. Oil for deep frying

Procedure:

  1. For the dough, mix all-purpose flour, semolina, ghee or oil, baking soda, and salt in a bowl.
  2. Add water gradually and knead into a firm yet smooth dough. Cover and let it rest for 15-20 minutes.
  3. For the filling, grind the soaked moong dal into a coarse paste without adding water.
  4. Heat oil in a pan. Add cumin seeds, fennel seeds, and grated ginger. Sauté until fragrant.
  5. Add the moong dal paste, turmeric powder, red chili powder, garam masala, and salt. Cook until the mixture thickens, stirring continuously.
  6. Remove from heat and let the filling cool. Add chopped coriander leaves.
  7. Divide the dough into small portions. Roll each portion into a small disc and place a spoonful of the moong dal filling in the center.
  8. Seal the edges to form a ball. Flatten slightly with your hands to form a disc-shaped kachori.
  9. Heat oil for deep frying. Fry the kachoris until they turn golden brown and crisp.
  10. Remove the kachoris from the oil and place them on paper towels to absorb excess oil.
  11. Serve the kachoris hot with mint chutney or tamarind sauce.
