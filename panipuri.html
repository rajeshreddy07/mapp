Panipuri Preparation

Ingredients:

  1. 1 cup semolina (sooji)
  2. 1/4 cup all-purpose flour (maida)
  3. 1/4 teaspoon baking soda
  4. Water, as needed, to make the dough
  5. For the filling:
  6. 1 cup boiled and mashed potatoes
  7. 1 cup cooked and mashed black chickpeas (kala chana)
  8. 1/2 cup chopped onions
  9. 1/2 cup chopped tomatoes
  10. Chopped coriander leaves for garnish
  11. For the Pani (spiced water):
  12. 1/2 cup chopped mint leaves
  13. 1/4 cup chopped coriander leaves
  14. 2-3 green chilies, chopped
  15. 1 teaspoon roasted cumin powder
  16. 1 teaspoon chaat masala
  17. 1 teaspoon tamarind paste
  18. Salt to taste
  19. 2 cups cold water

Procedure:

  1. For the Puri, mix semolina, all-purpose flour, baking soda, and enough water to form a stiff dough.
  2. Cover the dough and let it rest for 30 minutes.
  3. Divide the dough into small balls and roll them into small puris.
  4. Heat oil in a pan and deep fry the puris until they puff up and turn golden brown. Drain excess oil using a paper towel and set aside.
  5. For the filling, mix boiled and mashed potatoes, cooked black chickpeas, chopped onions, chopped tomatoes, and coriander leaves.
  6. For the Pani, blend mint leaves, coriander leaves, green chilies, roasted cumin powder, chaat masala, tamarind paste, and salt with cold water to form a spicy and tangy water.
  7. Make a small hole in the center of each puri and fill it with the potato-chickpea mixture.
  8. Pour the prepared Pani into each filled puri.
  9. Serve the Pani Puri immediately and enjoy the burst ofÂ flavors!
